Things to do and to avoid in Korea

  • Present and accept things with both hands
  • To salute, one hand is shaking, the other one is supporting your arm
  • Remove your shoes before entering in Korean households and temples
  • Always respect older people
  • Don't write in red
  • Don't place your chopesticks upright in your bowl
  • Avoid to refuse a proposition
  • Don't throw toilet paper in toilet
  • Don't pour your own drink

Top things to do in South Korea

1. Gyeongbokgung Palace

Built in 1395, the Gyeongbokgung Palace is the largest of the Five Palaces built in Seoul during the Joseon Dynasty (1392-1910). It is one of the grandest structures in the city and is a fine representation of Korean architecture.

Further information

2. Jeju Island

Jeju Island is a volcanic island located in the south of Korea. The highest mountain in South Korea, Hallasan (Halla Mountain), is located on Jeju Island. The peak of the mountain looks like a giant crater. The island has a climate that is temperate, with beautiful beaches and waterfalls.
